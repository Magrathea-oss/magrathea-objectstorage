[[quality-reactive-pipeline]]
=== Reactive Pipeline and Deterministic-chaos Quality Contract

[WARNING]
====
Status: *deferred / needs-validation while EP-10 remains active*. The 2026-07-14 link:../audits/2026-07-14-reactive-streaming-and-pipeline-chaos-source-audit.adoc[source audit] identifies static review items but does not replace the EP roadmap or revoke earlier semantic evidence. Signed authentication, SSE-S3, and named blocking/ownership paths require reproduction in supported Storage Engine compositions. The legacy in-memory adapter is not the owner-confirmed single-node/default backend, is isolated behind `legacy-in-memory-test`, and may be retired separately.
====

The quality tree for this roadmap is:

- *resource boundedness*: maximum frame bytes, demand/prefetch, concurrent stages, queued tasks/bytes, residual/cache state, retries, and fault delays are finite and compose into one reviewed budget;
- *thread responsiveness*: no filesystem/channel access, credential/policy reload, durable audit fsync, ACL access, cleanup, replica sink write, or unbounded CPU transform runs on a Reactor event-loop or gRPC callback thread;
- *resource safety*: every pooled buffer and file/channel lifecycle closes exactly once across success, error, cancellation, discard, over-limit, ignored-body, and injected-fault paths;
- *integrity truthfulness*: source, transformed, persisted, published, response-time, and at-rest integrity are named separately; one-open/single-pass GET remains explicit and no payload preflight is inferred;
- *determinism*: the same canonical plan and logical event sequence produce the same bounded decision trace despite concurrent scheduling; and
- *publication safety*: injected corruption cannot create a checksum-valid durable acknowledgement, bypass `W=2`, enable a degraded write, put payload in Ratis, or publish a reachable partial artifact.

P1 must freeze numeric frame, queue, concurrency, scheduler, and memory-budget values before normal-path P3 can validate them. Until then, the absence of a full-payload aggregator and the finiteness of every budget term are mandatory architecture gates, but no portable peak-memory or throughput number is claimed. ADR 0030 chaos evidence is final P4 and cannot substitute for P3.

=== Quality Scenarios

==== Scenario 1 — Repository-wide Object Upload Memory Safety

- **Status:** `planned / needs-validation`; current critical gaps are recorded in the dated source audit.
- **Context:** A client uploads objects through each applicable unsecured, exact-SigV4-signed, and SSE-S3 composition at the configured object-size and concurrency envelope.
- **Stimulus:** Sustain concurrent PUT streams, including slow producers/consumers and capacity rejection, without pre-materializing bodies in the runner.
- **Response:** No filter, repository, transform, or adapter retains a complete payload; demand and queues remain finite; rejection releases the offending buffer; no `OutOfMemoryError` or false success occurs.
- **Metric:** Measured payload retention stays below the P1-approved sum of per-stage frame/in-flight/residual/queue budgets plus a frozen runtime baseline and margin. The budget and environment manifest are prerequisites; no numeric pass result exists yet.

==== Scenario 11 — Fixed-Slice Quorum Write Safety

- **Status:** `implementation-informed`, `@implemented-and-validated` for `REQ-CLUSTER-002..004`; EP-10 remains partial.

- **Stimulus:** An unconditional whole-object write succeeds, has only one checksum-valid durable acknowledgement, or has two staged replicas but no control quorum.
- **Response:** S3 success occurs only after at least `W=2` checksum-valid durable acknowledgements and consensus commit of the object-reference generation. Otherwise publication fails, staged bytes remain unreachable, and no degraded result is returned.
- **Metric (2026-07-13):** The Java 21 shared real-process WebTestClient/AWS CLI gate passes 14 scenarios / 188 steps for `REQ-CLUSTER-001..005/019/020`; the first five publication scenarios retain their passing results. ==== Scenario 12 — Fixed-Slice Failover and Complete-Restart Integrity

- **Status:** `implementation-informed`, `@implemented-and-validated` for `REQ-CLUSTER-001`, `002`, and `005`.
- **Context:** Bucket and whole-object reference generations are committed in fixed A/B/C; stable UUIDs and each node's identity, Ratis, object, temporary, and runtime roots are distinct.
- **Stimulus:** Stop coordinator A while B and C remain mutually reachable, then retrieve through B; separately stop all A/B/C JVMs and restart them from their original non-empty roots with seed order `C,A,B`.

- **Metric (2026-07-13):** Both flows pass in the shared WebTestClient and AWS CLI real-process scenarios. The result proves one-node stop and complete fixed-cluster restart, not dynamic replacement, general partitions, healing, or production readiness.

==== Scenario 15 — Missing-local Repair Before GET Response (`REQ-CLUSTER-019`)

- **Status:** `implementation-informed`, `@implemented-and-validated`.

- **Stimulus:** An S3 client sends GET through B.

==== Scenario 16 — Corruption Failure and Later-read Recovery (`REQ-CLUSTER-020`)

- **Status:** `implementation-informed`, `@implemented-and-validated`.
- **Context:** The current reference names B and B has a present artifact whose corruption becomes visible only while the one-pass GET stream is consumed.
- **Stimulus:** A client reads through B, then a later client reads after repair completion.
- **Response:** The first GET fails with an integrity outcome, durably schedules/deduplicates repair, and never transparently retries another replica because bytes may already have been emitted. A fenced worker later verifies and durably replaces the artifact; only the later GET may succeed. Scheduling never converts the failed request into success.
- **Metric (2026-07-13):** `REQ-CLUSTER-020` passes in both real-process validation modes with client/body integrity assertions and no transparent corrupt-stream retry. The combined `001..005/019/020` gate passes 14 scenarios / 188 steps.

==== Scenario 17 — Durable-job Authority, Fencing, and Idempotent Recovery (`REQ-CLUSTER-021..025`)

- **Status:** `implementation-informed`; `021/022/023/024/025` are `@implemented-and-validated` within the bounded current-generation repair scope.
- **Context:** One repair job is deduplicated, transitions through consensus, or is interrupted around claim, transfer, durable publication, completion, snapshot, restart, or leader change.

==== Scenario 18 — Snapshot Migration and Fail-closed Compatibility (`REQ-CLUSTER-026`)
